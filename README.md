<h1>Problem(Inspiration)</h1>
<p>An estimated 20% of the ocean has been explored, meaning 80% of the ocean is unkown to science. The most problematic isses regarding exploration is depth, bringing freezing temperatures, no visibility, and insermountable amounts of pressure[1]. In fact, with every 10 meters dived, pressure is increased by around 1 atmosphere, meaning, if you went to the bottom of the Mariana Trench, nearly 7 miles (11265.4 meters) deep, you would have around 30000 NVIDIA Sheilds on top of you! We haven't been able to explore much of the ocean because of our lack of technology. There is only 1 active manned submersible, Deepsea Challenger, that can reach a depth of 11000m, with the second best, Jiao Long, reaching 7000m[2]. To unlock the scientific unkowns of the ocean, we must master the force of pressure deep under the sea.</p>
